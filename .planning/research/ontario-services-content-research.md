## Regulatory Context (Applies Province-Wide)

### Ontario Bill 60 — Fighting Delays, Building Faster Act (passed Nov 24, 2025)
- N4 non-payment notice period cut from 15 days to **7 days**
- Personal-use eviction (N12): 120-day notice, no longer requires tenant compensation or alternate accommodation
- Appeal window shortened from 30 days to **15 days**
- Tenants must pay 50% of alleged arrears to raise issues at LTB hearing
- Fixed-term leases no longer auto-convert to month-to-month (but tenancy still continues)
- Rent increase guideline: **2.5% for 2025**, **2.1% for 2026**
- 90-day written notice required before any rent increase
- One increase every 12 months maximum
- Rent control applies to units built **before November 15, 2018**

### LTB Wait Times (Province-Wide, 2025-2026)
- L1/L9 (non-payment): scheduled within approximately **3 months**
- All other applications: **5-7 months**
- LTB goal: 90-day life cycle for all applications
- Historical backlog from 2020-2024 still clearing; some older files pending
- Toronto-district LTB historically has highest volume and longest waits in province

### CRA Rental Income Reporting (2025)
- Form T776 required for all rental income
- CRA using AI data-matching (bank deposits, Airbnb listings, property registries) since 2025
- Records must be kept 6+ years
- Allowable deductions: mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, management fees, advertising
